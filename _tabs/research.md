My research focuses on using numerical simulations to study galaxy evolution. Right now, I'm working with [Professor Evan Schneider](https://evaneschneider.org/) on improving her hydrodynamics code, [Cholla](https://github.com/cholla-hydro/cholla), to make state-of-the-art isolated galaxy simulations. In particular, I am developing a dust model to include in Cholla that will allow us to study how the dust content of galaxies evolves over time and how dust interacts with other galaxy components. Cholla is uniquely positioned to answer this question because its GPU-native construction allows for kiloparsec-scale resolution, so we can see the dust physics as it takes place physically. Since we don't have to rely on sub-grid models, like many other codes that can't resolve the dust physics, we'll be taking the first look at what happens to dust in the various processes that shape, transform, and transport it in galaxies.
