# Simulating Dusty Clouds With Cholla
Observations and simulations have shown us that galactic outflows often contain many individual "clouds", or small, overdense regions of cooler temperatures than the rest of the outflow. In general, galactic winds are considered to be multi-phase, with a "hot" phase of $n\approx10^{-2.5}~\text{cm}^{-3}$ and $T\approx 10^{6.5}~\text{K}$ and a "cold" phase of $n\approx10^{2}~\text{cm}^{-3}$ and $T\approx 10^{2}~\text{K}$.
