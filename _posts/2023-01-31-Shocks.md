In fluid dynamics, a shock wave is a region where fluid dynamical variables vary rapidly. Those fluid variables are density, pressure, and velocity, and we can write an expression of them that treats the shock as a mathematical discontinuity. Conservation of mass, momentum, and energy give us the following:

\\( \rho_1 v_1 = \rho_2 v_2 \\)  (mass)      
\\( p_1 +\rho_1v_1^2 = p_2 + \rho_2v_2^2 \\)  (momentum)      
\\( \frac{1}{2}v_1^2+\frac{\gamma p_1}{(\gamma-1)\rho_1} = \frac{1}{2}v_2^2+\frac{\gamma p_2}{(\gamma-1)\rho_2} \\)  (energy)
